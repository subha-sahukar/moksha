Ganesha — Stories & Rituals

Introduction to Ganesha

Ganesha is the remover of obstacles and the god of beginnings. Devotees invoke him before major rites. He symbolizes wisdom (elephant head) and human life (the small body).

Selected stories

Parvati fashions Ganesha from turmeric paste; Shiva’s misunderstanding leads to the elephant head episode.

Ganesha serves as Vyasa’s scribe—an emblem of wisdom and literary patronage.

Moon mocked Ganesha; this story explains the reason for avoiding night travel on Ganesh Chaturthi for some.

Festivals & observances

Ganesh Chaturthi is the principal festival; family rituals, public processions, and idol immersion on final day are hallmark practices.
