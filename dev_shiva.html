Shiva — Myths, Tandava & Local Lore

Shiva in brief

Shiva is the ascetic and cosmic dancer—both fierce and compassionate. He is venerated across Shaiva traditions and central to many temple rituals.

Key myths

The Ananda Tandava describes the cycle of creation and destruction—the Nataraja icon symbolizes the rhythm of the cosmos.

Shiva drinks the poison Halahala to save the worlds—his throat turns blue (Neelakantha).

The story that leads to the Shakti Peetham myth—Sati’s self-immolation and Shiva's grief.

Temple practice & festivals

Mahashivaratri, Pradosham, and local month-specific rituals form the living rhythm of Shaiva worship. Padal Petra Sthalams are key hymn sites sung by Nayanmars.
