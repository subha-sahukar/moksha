Moksha Yatra

Jyotirlingas — Twelve Principal Shiva Shrines

Overview

Jyotirlingas are twelve sacred shrines where Shiva is said to have manifested as a column of light. Each has a unique historical and mythological background and is a major pilgrimage destination.

The 12 Jyotirlingas (summary)

  1. Somnath (Gujarat)
  2. Mallikarjuna (Srisailam, Andhra Pradesh)
  3. Mahakaleshwar (Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh)
  4. Omkareshwar (Madhya Pradesh)
  5. Kedarnath (Uttarakhand)
  6. Bhimashankar (Maharashtra)
  7. Rameshwaram / Ramanathaswamy (Tamil Nadu)
  8. Grishneshwar (Maharashtra)
  9. Vaidyanath (Deoghar) (Jharkhand)
  10. Trimbakeshwar (Maharashtra)
  11. Kashi Vishwanath (Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh)
  12. Ghrishneshwar / Others variant listing (region-specific)

Note: different textual traditions vary slightly in ordering; the list above covers canonical locations most pilgrimage guides include. Rameshwaram is often part of South-India-centric lists and is crucial to Tamil devotees.

Travel notes & rituals

  • Jyotirlinga pilgrimages are best planned by region — e.g., Maharashtra cluster (Bhimashankar, Trimbak, Grishneshwar), Central India (Omkareshwar, Mahakaleshwar).
  • Many Jyotirlinga shrines have strong seasonal constraints (Kedarnath: summer pilgrimage window). Check local timings and weather windows.
  • Rituals: abhishekam schedules, special pujas on Mondays and Mahashivaratri; observe local priest protocols.

Suggested circuits

Example: South-focused pilgrims often combine Srisailam (Mallikarjuna) → Srikalahasti (nearby) → Rameswaram; North pilgrims combine Kashi → Omkareshwar → Mahakaleshwar routes.
