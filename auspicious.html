Auspicious Events — Ekadashi, Pradosham, Shashti & more

Why temple calendars matter

Temple festivals and lunar/solar observances structure the devotional year. Many rituals (abhishekam, fasting, processions) are tied to lunar tithis and specific weekdays.

Key observances explained

Ekadashi (11th lunar day) — Vaishnava fasting day honoring Vishnu; many observe a day-long fast and special prayers at temples. There are 24 Ekadashis a year with names and unique characteristics.

Pradosham occurs twice a month (on the 13th lunar day) and is significant for Shiva worship—special abhishekam and rituals performed during the pradosha kala (twilight period).

A Pradosham falling on Saturday—considered potent for prayers to Saturn-related doshas; devotees seek specific remedies and rituals.

Shashti (6th lunar day) — important for Murugan worship; Skanda Sashti festival (month of Kartika) is widely celebrated.

Chaturthi (4th lunar day) — associated with Ganesha; Vinayaka Chaturthi is the major annual observance.

Amavasya (new moon) and Pournami (full moon) are monthly markers for ancestral rites, special pujas, and regional festivals (e.g., Kartik Purnima).

Full-moon circumambulation around sacred hills (e.g., Tiruvannamalai Girivalam) — a devotional night-walk combining austerity and community devotional practice.

Practical tips for pilgrims

  • Always check the temple’s official calendar for festival dates (they follow Tamil/solar-lunar specifics and may adjust for regional observances).
  • For Ekadashi/fasts: consult local priests for accepted dietary practices and fast-breaking procedures.
  • For Pradosham & Sani Pradosham: book morning/afternoon darshan early; twilight rituals attract high attendance.
