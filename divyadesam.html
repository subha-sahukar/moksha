Divya Desams — The 108 Sacred Vishnu Temples

What are the Divya Desams?

The Divya Desams are 108 temples of Lord Vishnu celebrated in the Tamil Vaishnava canon written by the Alvars (6th–9th centuries CE). Each Divya Desam is associated with sacred hymns (the Nalayira Divya Prabandham), an abiding tradition, and a unique temple form of Vishnu.

This page lists the Divya Desams by region, highlights important grouping (e.g., Nava Tirupati), and suggests pilgrimage circuits.

Quick circuits & how to use this page

  • South Tamil Nadu circuit: Nava Tirupati, Rameswaram nearby temples, Tirunelveli Divya Desams.
  • Central circuit (Chennai–Kumbakonam): Kanchipuram, Kumbakonam cluster, Srirangam.
  • Pan-India: Tirupati (Tirumala) is part of the Vaishnava pilgrimage circuit and historically visited along with Divya Desams.

Representative Divya Desams (selected highlights)

Deity: Venkateswara — One of the most visited Vaishnava shrines; famous for seva, tons of pilgrims, and ritual tradition.

Deity: Ranganatha — Largest functioning temple complex; Divya Desam and Vaikunta Ekadasi is major festival.

Deity: Sarangapani Perumal — Kumbakonam’s important Divya Desam and chariot festival.

Deity: Uppiliappan — Divya Desam and unique prasadam rules (salt-free).

The Nava Tirupati are nine Divya Desams on the Tamiraparani river; best visited as a circuit with river-side rituals.

Suggested pilgrimage templates

  • 3-day Kumbakonam & Thanjavur: Kumbakonam (Sarangapani → Adi Kumbeswarar → Nageswaran) → Darasuram (Airavatesvara) → Thanjavur (Brihadeeswarar).
  • 4-day South Tamil Nadu: Nava Tirupati circuit → Tirunelveli → Rameswaram (if extending to Ramar Path).
  • 10-day Vaishnava immersion: Srirangam → Kanchipuram → Kumbakonam → Tirupati → Srirangapatna / Udupi (if extending to Karnataka Vaishnava sites).

Notes & references

The Divya Desam corpus is textured with Alvar literature, regional festival calendars, and local priestly practices. For accuracy of festival dates check local temple boards; many festivals follow traditional lunar calendars (Tamil months, etc.).
