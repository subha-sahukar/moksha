Kumbakonam Temples – Detailed Listing

Moolavar: Sri Sarangapani Perumal (Vishnu)
Sthala Viruksham: Neem Tree (Azadirachta indica)
Significance: One of the tallest Rajagopurams among Divya Desams, associated with saint Nathamuni and home to one of the largest temple chariots.
Location: Kumbakonam Town, on banks of River Kaveri.
Unique Details: Includes Potramarai tank; major festivals during Tamil month Chittirai (Apr-May).

Moolavar: Chakrapani Perumal (Vishnu)
Sthala Viruksham: Banyan Tree
Significance: Unique Divya Desam with Vishnu worshipped in discus form; boasts temple architecture from Nayak period.
Location: Chakrapani Sannathi Street, Kumbakonam.
Unique Details: Statue of King Sarfoji II worshipping Vishnu present in temple.

Moolavar: Lord Shiva (Adi Kumbeswarar)
Sthala Viruksham: Ashoka Tree (Saraca asoca)
Significance: One of the oldest Shiva temples thought to be built in 7th century by Cholas.
Location: Thanjavur Main Road, Kumbakonam.
Unique Details: Famous for its unique Shiva Lingam and grand Chola style architecture.

Moolavar: Lord Shiva (Airavatesvara)
Sthala Viruksham: Margosa Tree (Neem)
Significance: UNESCO World Heritage Site; part of Great Living Chola Temples.
Location: Gurunathan Pillai Colony, Darasuram.
Unique Details: Known for exquisite stone carvings and massive chariot-shaped temple structure.

Moolavar: Lord Shiva (Nageswaran)
Sthala Viruksham: Neem Tree
Significance: Iconic Chola architecture built as a chariot; remarkable sunlight phenomena in March-April.
Location: Gandhi Adigal Salai, Kumbakonam.
Unique Details: Famous for serpent worship and unique annual festivals.

Moolavar: Lord Vishnu (Uppiliappan)
Sthala Viruksham: Neem Tree
Significance: Noted for salt-free prasad; Tamil canon Divya Prabandha glorifies the temple.
Location: Thirunageswaram, near Kumbakonam.
Unique Details: Place of marriage of Lord Vishnu with Bhoomidevi.

Moolavar: Lord Shiva (Mahalingeswarar)
Sthala Viruksham: Peepal Tree
Significance: One of seven major Shiva temples featuring the revered Jothimayalingam Lingam.
Location: SH 64, Thiruvidaimaruthur.
Unique Details: Active celebration of many local festivals and rituals.

Moolavar: Lord Rama
Sthala Viruksham: Neem Tree
Significance: Ancient temple housing idols of Rama’s brothers and Hanuman; coronation pose of Rama.
Location: East Street, Kumbakonam.
Unique Details: Spectacular wall paintings depicting Ramayana episodes.

Moolavar: Lord Vishnu (Vedanarayanaperumal) and Lord Brahma
Sthala Viruksham: Peepal Tree
Significance: One of only two temples dedicated to Lord Brahma in the world.
Location: Birman Kovil St, Kumbakonam.
Unique Details: Co-habitation with Vishnu and Lakshmi shrines.
