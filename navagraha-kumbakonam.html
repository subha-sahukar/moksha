Sūrya — The Sun

Light, sight, authority. Begin the yatra in radiance.

Mini-legend: A king consumed by pride humbled himself with sunrise arghya here; vision—inner and outer—returned. The Sun rewards humble illumination.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ ह्रां ह्रीं ह्रौं सः सूर्याय नमः
  • Offer water at sunrise facing east (Arghya).
  • Red flowers, wheat, light a sesame-oil lamp.
  • Devote: recite the nama 9/27/108 times.

Temple & Timing

Suryanar Kovil — best at dawn; arrive 30 minutes before sunrise for unobstructed arghya.

Darshan tip: avoid wearing dark sunglasses; stand quietly while offering water.

Chandra — The Moon

Mind, emotions, nurturing — calm the tides of heart.

Mini-legend: Chandra's attachment and imbalance found cure by Shiva's grace here; Thingalur teaches equanimity through humble offerings.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ श्रीं चन्द्राय नमः
  • Milk abhishekam and white flowers.
  • Evening pradakshina under moonlight when possible.
  • Practice quiet breathing after offering.

Temple & Timing

Thingalur — ideal at evening / moonrise. Carry a small vessel for milk offering.

Darshan tip: perform one slow circumambulation; avoid loud conversations.

Maṅgala — Mars (Vaitheeswaran)

Strength, protection, healing — Mars as medicine.

Mini-legend: Vaitheeswaran Koil blends physician-lore with Mars energy: wounds are addressed by ritual, medicinal practice and disciplined vows.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ क्रां क्रीं क्रौं सः भौमाय नमः
  • Offer sesame oil lamps and red cloth.
  • Carry out simple vows; consult the temple for folk remedies.
  • Combine pratishtha rituals with medical care when needed.

Temple & Timing

Vaitheeswaran Koil — mornings recommended. Inform priest about the nature of your request for appropriate pariharam.

Darshan tip: tie a small sacred thread at designated posts if instructed.

Budha — Mercury (Thiruvenkadu)

Speech, intellect, commerce — steady the mind's instruments.

Mini-legend: Shiva steadied Budha's confused identity here — clarity of speech and thought is the gift.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ ब्रीं बुधाय नमः
  • Green offerings, donate books or stationery.
  • Practice a short teaching/vocal exercise after darshan.
  • Offer clarity of intention before debates or exams.

Temple & Timing

Thiruvenkadu — daytime; quiet mornings are good for study-related petitions.

Darshan tip: carry a small notebook and note a vow to teach or share learning.

Guru — Jupiter (Alangudi)

Wisdom, guidance, protector of dharma.

Mini-legend: Guru's selfless act to save worlds is remembered here — long-term guidance and steady protection are the blessings.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ ग्रां गुरवे नमः
  • Yellow flowers, turmeric, feeding students/teachers.
  • Seek blessings for education, marriage foundations, and children.
  • Offer time to teach or mentor someone.

Temple & Timing

Alangudi — best on Thursdays or during morning seva.

Darshan tip: perform a small act of learning (read a pasuram) and offer it mentally as prasadam.

Śukra — Venus (Kanjanur)

Harmony, relationship, artistry.

Mini-legend: Shukra's austerities and refinement are honoured here; beauty aligned with discipline is his gift.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ द्रां शुक्राय नमः
  • White fragrant flowers, soft music, ghee lamp on Fridays.
  • Offer art (song, composition) or support an artist.
  • Keep household offerings neat & fragrant.

Temple & Timing

Kanjanur — daytime and festival hours. Friday favored for relationship petitions.

Darshan tip: keep gifts modest & meaningful rather than flashy.

Śani — Saturn (Thirunallar)

Karma, trials, long-term strength.

Mini-legend: Śani's severity is tempered by devotion: Ganesha and Hanuman are woven into folk cures that ease Shani's harsher lessons.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ प्रां शनैश्चराय नमः
  • Sesame oil lamps, give black sesame, hands-on service to elders.
  • Regular steadiness: repeated vows and quiet service hold more than one-time rituals.
  • Pair with Hanuman recitation where local practice suggests it.

Temple & Timing

Thirunallar — Saturday mornings are busiest; plan early or consult the priest for special pariharam.

Darshan tip: do not rush — Shani pariharam benefits from time and routine.

Rāhu — Ascending Node (Thirunageswaram)

Transformation, sudden change, redemption.

Mini-legend: Rahu's hunger at the Amrita episode leads to the head-and-tail story. Here the famous milk abhishekam tale teaches that even strange phenomena can be sanctified into blessing.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ भ्रां राहवे नमः
  • Milk abhishekam performed under priest guidance.
  • Offer blue/black flowers or mixed grains as directed.
  • Avoid improvisation — follow temple's custom for Rahu rituals.

Temple & Timing

Thirunageswaram — special abhishekams on festival days; check priest availability.

Darshan tip: consult archakar for Rahu-specific timings (Rahu Kalam cautions).

Ketu — The Tail (Keezhperumpallam)

Detachment, insight, moksha.

Mini-legend: At Keezhperumpallam (also called Vanagiri), Ketu worshipped Shiva to remove sins after the cosmic churning. This quiet place is about inner release and steady practice.

Beeja & Nama — ॐ स्त्रां केतवे नमः
  • Multi-grain offerings, lamp-lighting and naga worship.
  • Make a small vow and return to it each day; inner practice strengthens temple work.
  • Combine quiet meditation with the external rite for deeper effect.

Temple & Timing

Keezhperumpallam — typically open 6am–12pm & 4pm–8:30pm (verify locally). Follow archakar guidance for Ketu rites.

Darshan tip: bring mixed grains if planning a dedicated offering; maintain quiet after puja.
