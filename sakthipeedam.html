Shakti Peethams — Devotional geography of the Goddess

Overview

Shakti Peethams are shrines believed to have originated from the body parts of the goddess Sati after Shiva’s tandava. There are many regional counts (some list 51, others more); each is associated with a body part and a unique goddess form.

Major Shakti Peethas (selected)

  • Srisailam (Mallikarjuna & Bhramaramba) — Andhra Pradesh: both Shiva and Shakti are worshipped here.
  • Kamakhya — Assam: tantric center and grand Ambubachi festival.
  • Vishalakshi (Varanasi) — Uttar Pradesh.
  • Other regional peethams — many are listed across peninsular India and the subcontinent.

Pilgrimage notes

Shakti pilgrimages are often linked to feminine festivals (Navaratri, Ambubachi), and many peethams emphasize specific tantric and mother-goddess rituals—seek local guidance before attending esoteric rites.
