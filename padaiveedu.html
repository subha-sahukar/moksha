Arupadai Veedu — The Six Abodes of Lord Murugan

What are Arupadai Veedu?

The Arupadai Veedu are six temples associated with Lord Murugan (Kartikeya) — each temple marks an important episode in Murugan’s life and the Tamil bhakti tradition.

The Six Abodes

Murugan’s marriage to Deivayanai; cave shrine with ancient sculptures.

Sea-front victory shrine where Murugan defeats Surapadman.

The hill shrine of renunciation and devotion; famous for the sacred hill and devotees carrying kavadis.

Murugan teaches the meaning of Om to Lord Shiva — a place of learning and devotion.

Homecoming shrine where peace and reunion are celebrated.

Forest shrine connected to fertility and divine bliss with consorts Valli & Deivayanai.

Pilgrimage advice

  • Best to plan Murugan circuits during Skanda Sashti month and local temple festivals.
  • Combine Tiruchendur with Tuticorin coastal attractions and Palani with nearby hill circuits.
